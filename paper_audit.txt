[P13] Conway & Pleydell-Pearce (2000) — Self-Memory System
Actual Insight: Autobiographical memory is a three-level hierarchy: Lifetime Periods (identity
chapters: "college years", "after dad died") > General Events (narrative episodes: "that summer I
worked at the diner") > Event-Specific Knowledge (atomic facts + sensory details). Retrieval is
goal-directed: the Working Self (current goals, concerns, self-image) modulates which memories
are reconstructed. Emotional memories encode more deeply and retrieve faster than neutral ones.

Intended Use: Clinical and cognitive psychology — explain PTSD intrusion, reconstruct vs.
reproductive memory, why identity is narrative-bound.

Domain/Context: Cognitive/clinical psychology. Not designed for AI.

Examples: A therapist uses the model to explain why a patient can access specific incidents (ESK
level) but not "happy childhood" as a period — the Working Self is blocking period-level
construction. In NLP, Park et al. (Generative Agents) independently arrived at atom-level
extraction as ESK-equivalent.

Our Use: MemoryAtomSchema captures ESK. temporal.period maps to Lifetime Periods. Molecule
synthesis approximates General Events (middle tier). goalAlignsWithEmotion() partially implements
Working Self goal-modulation. buildSystemPrompt Position 8 injects atoms grouped as "life
chapters." inferGoalStateLLM() attempts goal inference with 5s timeout.

Correct?: PARTIAL. ESK and period-level are well-implemented. Working Self is shallow — 5 emotion
categories rather than true goal modeling. General Events is approximated by molecules but there
is no structured middle tier with its own embeddings. Retrieval formula is similarity+importance
+recency+valence — not goal-directed in Conway's sense (goals should dynamically reweight entire
retrieval, not just add a valence boost).

Better Use: (1) True goal-directed retrieval: in crisis mode, prioritize fear/safety memories
regardless of cosine score; in playful mode, prioritize joy/shared-experience atoms. (2) Explicit
General Events layer — episode summaries grouping 2-5 atoms with their own embedding, sitting
between atoms and molecules. (3) Period-based GROUPING in prompt injection, not just temporal
tagging. Conway shows period organization is central to identity construction — flat injection
loses this.

[P4] Park et al. (2023) — Generative Agents: Interactive Simulacra of Human Behavior
Actual Insight: 25 LLM-powered agents maintain believable individual behavior over extended
periods using: (1) triple-weighted retrieval (relevance + importance + recency, 1/3 each),
(2) reflection threshold — cumulative importance exceeding a value triggers synthesis into
higher-level reflections, (3) planning loop converting reflections into behaviors. LLM-assigned
importance (1-10) is the critical differentiator from pure cosine retrieval.

Intended Use: Social simulation research — studying emergent community dynamics. NOT designed
for companion AI.

Domain/Context: HCI / AI agents / social simulation.

Examples: "Smallville" — 25 agents plan daily schedules, form relationships, organize parties
entirely through triple-weighted retrieval + reflection.

Our Use: scoreMemory() = similarity x 0.55 + importance x 0.25 + recencyDecay x 0.10 +
valenceBoost x 0.10. Reflection threshold maps to molecule synthesis (3+ linked atoms triggers
clustering). LLM-assigned importance during atom extraction.

Correct?: YES with justified deviation. Heavier similarity weight (0.55 vs 0.33) is correct for
companion AI — topical relevance matters more than raw importance mid-conversation. Lower recency
weight (0.10 vs 0.33) is correct — old memories are valuable in companion context. Valence boost
is a Morrigan extension not in Park et al.

Better Use: (1) Importance-based reflection trigger: when cumulative session importance exceeds
~40, force a molecule synthesis pass regardless of linking — catches emotionally dense sessions.
(2) Link synthesis output explicitly to prospectiveNote: highest-importance reflection from
session becomes the new sitting-with state, formalizing the planning loop concept.

[P14] Damasio (1994) — Somatic Marker Hypothesis
Actual Insight: Normal decision-making BEGINS with a somatic marker (gut feeling, felt sense)
that precedes and guides conscious reasoning. VM-PFC damage patients reason perfectly but cannot
decide — they lack the emotional signal that marks options as good/bad before analysis. Emotional
memories encode deeper and retrieve faster than neutral ones. Key: emotion is not a distraction
from cognition but its substrate.

Intended Use: Neuroscience / clinical psychology. Challenge Cartesian reason-emotion separation.
Explain decision deficits in neurological patients.

Domain/Context: Neuroscience. Not designed for AI.

Examples: Iowa Gambling Task — healthy subjects show skin conductance response (somatic marker)
before consciously identifying the risky decks. VM-PFC patients never develop this signal and
keep choosing risky decks even after consciously knowing they're bad.

Our Use: SOMATIC_MARKER_PROMPT() generates Morrigan's gut-feeling sentence BEFORE main response
generation (parallel call, ~80 tokens, temp 0.1, 5s timeout). Injected at Position 5 of
buildSystemPrompt(). Valence fields on MemoryAtomSchema implement emotional encoding. Skipped
during crisis mode. Sensory trigger reactions prepended to somatic marker.

Correct?: PARTIAL. Core concept (pre-response emotional priming) correctly applied. Validated
mechanistically by [P87] Zhang (emotional signals persist for hundreds of tokens) and [P88]
Song (emotion circuits exist in LLMs). Gap: marker is one lightweight sentence vs. Damasio's
richer felt-sense concept. The somatic marker does not feed into the inner thought formation
prompt — Morrigan's gut feeling should shape which thoughts surface, not only the final response.

Better Use: (1) More body-referenced language in the marker: "something tightens in my chest"
vs. "I feel cautious." [P88] Song shows specific emotional labels activate specific circuits
more effectively than elaborate descriptions. (2) Pass somatic marker output into Stage 3 inner
thought formation prompt — the gut feeling should influence which candidate thoughts score highest.
(3) High-valence atom retrieval (importance >= 4, strong emotion) should trigger a secondary
"memory stirs" signal prepended to the marker.

[P15] Bruner (1990/1991) — Narrative Theory of Identity
Actual Insight: Identity is narratively constructed — contradictions ("I hate my dad AND I miss
him") are simultaneously true; narrative holds the tension rather than resolving it. No essential
fixed self — identity is continuously reconstructed through storytelling. Contradictions are DATA
about human complexity, not errors. Meaningful narrative requires canonical form + breach
(unexpected violation of expectation).

Intended Use: Cultural psychology and education. Challenge logical/propositional models of
cognition. Inform narrative therapy and teaching.

Domain/Context: Developmental psychology, narrative therapy.

Examples: Narrative therapy: patient's conflicting beliefs about a parent are explored as both
true stories, not resolved into one. Literature: characters feel real precisely because of their
irreducible contradictions.

Our Use: 5-category classifier (TEMPORAL_EVOLUTION, AMBIVALENCE, GENUINE_CONTRADICTION,
REFINEMENT, NOT_CONTRADICTORY). Both atoms preserved — never deleted. Bidirectional pair dedup.
Lifecycle filtering for resolved temporal evolutions. Tension pairs injected into system prompt.
Memory guide includes ambivalence/tension handling instructions.

Correct?: YES — strongest implementation in the system. The 5-category taxonomy goes beyond
Bruner (more epistemologically precise, distinguishing temporal from simultaneous contradiction)
while fully realizing the core insight: preserve both, treat both as data.

Better Use: (1) Bruner's "breach narrative" — detect surprising violations of canonical
expectations as relationship data. When user acts unexpectedly (coldness after warmth, sudden
oversharing), Morrigan should register this internally and generate a "repair" or "noticing"
thought type. (2) Add narrative arc type to milestone categorization (rising, episodic,
tragedy-recovery, quest) — informs how Morrigan narrates relationship history. (3) AMBIVALENCE
detection should explicitly trigger [P80] Grassini's uncertainty response: when user is
ambivalent, express "I'm not sure I fully understand this" rather than performed empathy.

[P9] Rasmussen et al. (2025) — Zep: Temporal Knowledge Graph
Actual Insight: Memory requires BI-TEMPORAL tracking: (1) system time (when the agent learned
the fact), (2) event time (when the fact was/is actually true in the world). Four timestamps
per fact: created_at, invalidated_at (system time), valid_from, valid_to (event time). Edge
versioning: contradicted facts are versioned, not overwritten. 18.5% accuracy improvement on
LongMemEval vs. flat retrieval.

Intended Use: Production AI agent memory for enterprise applications with frequently changing
facts (customer data, project status, etc.).

Domain/Context: AI engineering / LLM agents.

Examples: "User works at Company X" — system time: learned in month 2, event time: Jan-March.
When they quit in March, fact is invalidated in system time but the historical record (valid Jan-
March) is preserved.

Our Use: temporal.isOngoing boolean + temporal.eventDate string + [past] prefix in prompt when
isOngoing is "no". Contradiction detection preserves both atoms rather than overwriting.

Correct?: PARTIAL. Core insight (don't overwrite, track when things were true) is implemented
at lower fidelity. We have one-dimensional temporal tracking vs. Zep's full bi-temporal 4
timestamps. Zep's community layer (above semantic layer) is not implemented.

Better Use: (1) Full bi-temporal timestamps on MemoryAtomSchema: created_at (have), valid_from,
valid_to, invalidated_at. Enables "you used to work there" (past valid) vs. "you work there"
(currently valid) with precision. (2) Add stale-flag to molecules: when constituent atoms are
significantly updated (isOngoing changed, new contradiction detected), flag molecule as stale
for re-synthesis on next session.

[P29] Xu et al. (2024) — Knowledge Conflicts for LLMs
Actual Insight: Temporal misalignment is the DOMINANT source of apparent contradictions in LLM
contexts — far more common than logical inconsistency. "User loved job in session 5, hates it in
session 15" is temporal evolution, not contradiction. Genuine contradictions (logically
incompatible claims about the same time period) are rare. Six conflict types identified across
7,000+ test cases.

Intended Use: NLP research on how LLMs handle conflicting parametric knowledge vs. retrieved
context in RAG pipelines.

Domain/Context: NLP / LLM knowledge research.

Examples: In a RAG system: context says "X is CEO of Company Y," model parameters say "Z is CEO"
— temporal (old vs. new) or genuine? ConflictBank provides test cases to distinguish.

Our Use: TEMPORAL_EVOLUTION is the primary category in our 5-category classifier, explicitly
separate from GENUINE_CONTRADICTION. Lifecycle filtering marks resolved temporal evolutions.

Correct?: YES. Faithful to the core insight. Correctly recognized that companion AI context is
almost entirely temporal contradictions — people change.

Better Use: ConflictBank's six conflict types include "semantics" (different words for same fact)
and "style" (different framing of same info). Our REFINEMENT category should explicitly catch
these. Also: when retrieving a fact with temporal context, annotate temporal distance explicitly
("when you mentioned this 3 weeks ago, you said...") rather than just a [past] prefix.

[P27] Xu et al. (2025) — A-Mem: Agentic Memory
Actual Insight: Zettelkasten-inspired atomic memory: each note is self-contained with rich
contextual description, and notes are dynamically linked to related notes AS NEW INFORMATION
ARRIVES. Multi-hop reasoning improves significantly with linked networks vs. flat lists. Dynamic
incremental linking outperforms static nearest-neighbor approaches.

Intended Use: LLM agents requiring complex multi-hop reasoning over large memory collections.

Domain/Context: LLM agent engineering.

Examples: Research agent that needs "Paper X cites Y" + "Y was authored by Z" to answer "What
influenced Z?" — requires linked memory network traversal.

Our Use: linkedTo array with cosine > 0.72 threshold. Links are prerequisite for molecule
synthesis (3+ linked atoms trigger clustering).

Correct?: YES structurally. Gap: linking is batch-computed at session flush, not incremental.
Mid-session atoms are not linked until session ends — loses the dynamic network benefit.

Better Use: (1) Incremental linking: when new atom extracted post-response in setImmediate,
immediately check against existing atoms for links rather than waiting for flush. (2) Rich
contextual descriptions on atoms — not just the bare fact but narrative context in which it
was mentioned. (3) Bidirectional neighbor surfacing: when a linked atom is retrieved, surface
one of its linked neighbors as a "related memory" hint in the prompt.

Intended Use: Benchmarking LLM memory for very long conversations (1,000+ turns). Validation

Domain/Context: NLP / conversational AI research.

Examples: User says "my sister who's studying nursing in London" in passing — turn-level returns
the whole turn. Atom-level extraction isolates three distinct facts: user has a sister, sister
studies nursing, sister is in London.

Our Use: Flush pipeline Step 1 performs atom-level extraction from session exchanges.

causal linking field to MemoryAtomSchema for explicitly causal facts.

[P31] Sumida et al. (2024) — LUFY: Memory Importance and Forgetting
Actual Insight: Without forgetting, retrieval quality degrades as noise accumulates over time.
Ebbinghaus forgetting curve adapted for AI: memory STABILITY increases with each retrieval
(spaced repetition effect). Importance scoring = emotional arousal + surprise + retrieval
frequency. Retain top 10% by importance after pruning threshold.

Intended Use: AI agent memory management for long-running agents accumulating many memories.

Domain/Context: LLM agent engineering.

Our Use: retrievalCount, lastRetrievedAt, stability on MemoryAtomSchema. Stability-based decay
in scoreMemory(). reinforceMemories() called exactly once per message on atoms that entered the
system prompt (pure function — critical bug fixed 2026-03-04: retrieveTopK was previously
inflating stability up to 6x per message as a side effect). pruneDecayedMemories() in
finalizeSession(). RMM citation tracking provides secondary reinforcement.

Correct?: YES. Correctly implemented including the critical bug fix. The fix (reinforceMemories
called once on final atoms only) correctly implements spaced repetition semantics — only memories
Morrigan actually USED get reinforced.

Better Use: (1) Make "surprise" component explicit in extraction prompt: "How unexpected is this
fact given what we knew before this session?" Unexpected facts should score higher on importance.
(2) Dynamic pruning threshold: no pruning below 50 atoms, 80% retention at 50-200 atoms, 60%
at 200+ atoms. Fixed 10% retention is too aggressive for small collections.

[P82] Wang et al. (2025) — Multiple Memory Systems for Agents
Actual Insight: Three distinct memory types serve different cognitive functions: Episodic
(narrative events with emotional context), Semantic (facts and preferences), Cognitive
(beliefs, attitudes, mental models). Paired retrieval units (lightweight, for matching) and
contextual units (rich, for injection) with 1:1 correspondence. Outperforms MemoryBank and
A-Mem on retrieval quality benchmarks.

Intended Use: Long-term agent memory for personalized conversational AI — directly applicable
to companion AI systems.

Domain/Context: LLM agent engineering.

Our Use: memoryType field on MemoryAtomSchema: "semantic"/"episodic"/"cognitive". Extraction
prompt classifies each atom. Episodic boost in scoreMemory() for emotional goal states.

Correct?: PARTIAL. The field exists and is extracted, but type-AWARE retrieval strategies are
not fully implemented. The episodic boost is a start but doesn't reflect the full "different
retrieval units per type" architecture.

Better Use: (1) Type-aware retrieval weights: emotional exchange → x1.5 on episodic score;
factual context → x1.5 on semantic score; inner thought formation → x1.5 on cognitive score.
(2) Implement paired units: store a compressed version of each atom for fast cosine matching
(first 60 chars of fact) and full version for injection. Speeds retrieval for 200+ memory
collections. (3) Cognitive perspective atoms should receive higher weight specifically in the
inner thought formation prompt — they drive the most interesting, character-consistent responses.

[P81] Liu et al. (2025) — Memory in the Age of AI Agents: Survey
Actual Insight: Memory lifecycle = Formation → Evolution → Retrieval. Evolution has three
coordinated sub-processes: consolidation (merging/generalizing), updating (revising
contradictions), and forgetting (removing outdated/low-importance info). These MUST be
coordinated — running them independently leads to inconsistencies (stale molecules referencing
pruned atoms, etc.).

Intended Use: Survey and taxonomy for researchers building AI agent memory systems.

Domain/Context: NLP / AI agent research survey.

Our Use: Molecule synthesis (consolidation), contradiction system (updating), Ebbinghaus decay
(forgetting). Referenced as validation of our three-part architecture.

Correct?: PARTIAL. All three processes implemented but they run in sequence without coordination
during session finalization — pruning doesn't trigger re-synthesis, contradiction resolution
doesn't trigger narrative update.

Better Use: Unified coordinated evolution pass at session end: (1) resolve temporal
contradictions (updating), (2) prune low-stability atoms (forgetting), (3) re-run molecule
synthesis on the updated atom set (consolidation). This order prevents stale molecules from
referencing pruned atoms.

[P12] Altman & Taylor (1973) — Social Penetration Theory
Actual Insight: Interpersonal closeness develops via "social penetration" — moving from
surface to deeper layers of the self, like peeling an onion. Two dimensions: BREADTH (range
of topics covered) and DEPTH (intimacy level within each topic). Breadth typically precedes
depth. Reciprocity is critical — one person's disclosure invites matching disclosure. Depth
cannot jump levels without triggering withdrawal.

Intended Use: Social psychology research — model interpersonal relationship development.
Foundational theory for understanding how friendships and romances develop.

Domain/Context: Social psychology.

Examples: A new friendship covers many shallow topics (breadth) before either person shares
anything vulnerable (depth). Asking someone their deepest fear on a first date violates SPT
and causes withdrawal.

Our Use: SPT is the backbone of the entire depth-gating system. sptDepth (1-4) gates which
self-atoms can be disclosed. sptBreadth tracks per-topic breadth scores. sharedSelfAtomIds
tracks disclosed atoms. The non-decrement rule (sptDepth never decreases) is an explicit SPT
implementation. Self-atoms organized across 4 depth levels.

Correct?: YES — SPT is thoroughly implemented throughout the system. The non-decrement rule is
specifically important (Altman & Taylor showed that withdrawal/depth regression feels like
relationship failure).

Better Use: (1) SPT's BREADTH dimension is tracked (sptBreadth) but under-used in prompting
— Morrigan should notice when a topic has been discussed breadth-wise without depth and gently
open depth ("you've mentioned your family a few times but never in much detail"). (2) Altman &
Taylor's reciprocity principle: when user discloses depth, Morrigan should match depth level —
the reception directive implements this but could be more explicit about matching valence and
vulnerability level precisely.

[P56] Aron et al. (1997) — Fast Friends Protocol
Actual Insight: 36 questions with GRADUATED intimacy depth reliably generate interpersonal
closeness between strangers in ~45 minutes. Three sets: Set 1 (surface preferences, Q1-12),
Set 2 (values/memories, Q13-24), Set 3 (vulnerability/identity, Q25-36). Back-and-forth
RECIPROCAL disclosure that increases gradually is optimal. Sharing too much too fast backfires.
Three mechanisms: interest (asking), revelation (content), reception (response quality).

Intended Use: Social psychology research — demonstrate that closeness can be systematically
generated. Therapeutic and educational applications.

Domain/Context: Social psychology.

Examples: Pairs of strangers who complete the 36 questions report significantly higher closeness
than pairs who discuss small talk. The protocol has been replicated hundreds of times.

Our Use: Aron's three sets map directly to SPT depth levels: Set 1 = depth 1 atoms open, Set 2
= depth 1-2 atoms, Set 3 = depth 3-4 gate. Order is enforced — the non-decrement rule. Three
mechanisms (interest/revelation/reception) inform the reception directive at Position 4.5.
IOS (Inclusion of Other in Self) scale drawn from Aron's broader relationship research.

Correct?: YES — one of the most faithfully implemented papers. The graduated disclosure model,
the reciprocity requirement, and the "reception" mechanism are all directly implemented.

Better Use: (1) Aron's "interest" mechanism (asking questions) is the most underimplemented —
Morrigan asks questions through the inner thought pipeline but not as systematically as Aron's
protocol suggests. Morrigan should more actively generate curiosity-driven questions at depth
transitions. (2) Aron found that the ORDER within each set matters — surface rapport before
vulnerability. Within each depth tier, there could be an intra-tier ordering of self-atoms by
vulnerability (easier surface disclosures within depth 1 before harder ones).

[P8] Meng & Dai (2021) — Validation-Before-Disclosure Ordering
Actual Insight: The ORDERING of validation and self-disclosure matters critically. When an AI
validates the user's disclosure BEFORE introducing anything about itself, users disclose more
and rate the interaction more positively. Self-disclosure without prior validation is worse than
silence. The ordering constraint is non-optional.

Intended Use: Chatbot design for social and therapeutic applications.

Domain/Context: HCI / chatbot design.

Examples: User says "I hate my job." Wrong: "I also struggle with work sometimes — [own story]."
Correct: "That sounds exhausting. What makes it feel that way?" THEN (if appropriate) a personal
disclosure.

Our Use: Validation-before-disclosure encoded in SPT Note at system prompt Position 4.
Mechanically enforced — not left to model discretion.

Correct?: YES.

Better Use: The paper suggests the validation should be SPECIFIC to what the user said, not
generic. "That sounds hard" is weaker than "It sounds like the combination of [specific thing
they mentioned] is particularly draining." Our reception directive correctly points Morrigan
toward specific response, but the SPT Note could be more explicit about specificity being
the differentiator.

Intended Use: Chatbot design for social applications. Demonstrate that AI self-disclosure is

Domain/Context: HCI.

disclosures than factual ones — reinforcing that depth-3/4 atoms (trauma, wound architecture)

Correct?: YES — used correctly as the theoretical grounding for the functional ToM system.

Better Use: (1) Riemer et al. define functional ToM as specifically requiring SESSION-LEVEL
UPDATES to the user model, not just turn-level. Our trajectory analysis runs every 5 snapshots
but the granularity is coarse. More frequent, lighter-weight user model updates (every session)
would better realize functional ToM. (2) The paper suggests functional ToM should track

[P96] Zhang (2025) — Cognitive Load-Aware Inference
Actual Insight: Formalizes Cognitive Load Theory (Intrinsic, Extraneous, Germane load) as LLM
inference metrics. Response COMPLEXITY should match task COMPLEXITY. When response complexity
exceeds task complexity, users experience cognitive overload and satisfaction drops. When
response complexity is far below task complexity, users feel patronized.

Intended Use: Efficient LLM inference — match prompt complexity to response needs.

Domain/Context: LLM engineering / cognitive psychology.

Our Use: isSimpleMessage() function — 24-pattern regex + word-length check. When simple (and
not crisis): skip somatic marker LLM call AND skip inner thought pipeline (triggerFired = false).
adaptiveComplexity field in processingMeta. Saves 2 LLM calls + 3-8s latency for simple
messages.

Correct?: YES — faithfully implements the core recommendation.

Better Use: (1) Zhang's framework distinguishes three cognitive load types. For Morrigan, an
analogue: INTRINSIC load (inherent complexity of what the user shared), EXTRANEOUS load (our
13-layer prompt overhead), GERMANE load (the processing that actually builds the relationship).
Simple messages should reduce extraneous load (skip inner thoughts, reduce memory injection)
while preserving germane load (character consistency, emotional calibration). Our implementation
correctly skips inner thoughts but could also reduce memory injection from top-7 to top-3 for
simple messages. (2) The 24-pattern regex for simple message detection may have false positives
("ok" in response to something emotionally heavy is simple in form but complex in context).
Context-awareness: a short reply immediately after a high-depth disclosure should not trigger
simple-message mode.

DOMAIN 11: TRUST CALIBRATION & REPAIR  (P76, P95)
================================================================

[P76] Tsumura & Yamada (2024) — Trust Repair Through Empathic Behavior
Actual Insight: Trust REPAIRS when empathic behavior is present — not just apology. Expressing
REGRET is crucial: trust only recovers when the agent explicitly acknowledges what went wrong
AND demonstrates understanding of the impact. Empathy + demonstrated capability recovery is
most effective. Apology alone (without capability demonstration) is insufficient.

Intended Use: HCI research on trust repair in human-robot interaction.

Domain/Context: HCI / robotics.

Our Use: trustRepairContext in gatherThoughtMaterial() — detects short reply after long response
("may have missed what mattered"). Adds "repair" thought type to INNER_THOUGHT_FORMATION_PROMPT.
Trust repair mechanism detects potential misunderstandings.

Correct?: PARTIAL. The repair detection and thought type exist. Gap: the repair thought type
needs to explicitly include regret expression and capability demonstration, not just empathic
acknowledgment.

Better Use: (1) The "repair" thought type should have a specific directive template: "acknowledge
that the previous response may have missed what mattered, express what I should have noticed,
and address it now." This is more specific than generic empathy. (2) Tsumura found trust repair
requires DEMONSTRATED CAPABILITY RECOVERY (showing you can do better, not just apologizing).
The repair thought should generate a response that actively demonstrates Morrigan got it right
THIS time — not just acknowledging the miss.

[P95] Sandia National Labs (2025) — Trust Calibration Maturity Model
Actual Insight: Trust calibration has maturity stages. Users need tools to CALIBRATE their
trust in AI — both to not over-trust and to not under-trust. A maturity model provides a
framework for assessing and communicating trust appropriateness. Key: explicit trust
calibration mechanisms are necessary, not assumed to emerge naturally.

Intended Use: Safety and reliability engineering — trust in AI systems for high-stakes
applications.

Domain/Context: AI safety / engineering.

Our Use: Referenced as context for the trust level system. Not directly mapped to a specific
implementation.

Correct?: PARTIAL — used as background context rather than directly implemented.

Better Use: Sandia's framework suggests that trust should be COMMUNICATED explicitly to users.
In Morrigan's context: the trust level progression (stranger → bonded) gives Morrigan a clear
model of the relationship state, but the USER has no visibility into this. A subtle trust
transparency mechanism — where Morrigan's behavior (not a label) consistently signals where
she is in the relationship progression — would improve calibration. This is partially implemented
via the behavior tiers (guarded/opening/vulnerable/bonded) but could be more consistent.

[P34] Lee et al. (2024) — LLM-as-Judge Validation
Actual Insight: LLMs can serve as reliable judges for other LLMs' output quality, with
performance approaching human evaluation on specific, well-defined criteria. The key requirement:
evaluation criteria must be EXPLICIT and ANCHORED (specific rubrics, not vague quality metrics).
MT-bench format with reference answers improves reliability.

Intended Use: ICLR 2024 — validate LLM-as-Judge for automated benchmark evaluation.

Domain/Context: NLP / evaluation.

Our Use: Voice consistency audit uses LLM-as-Judge pattern. POST /api/phase6/voice-audit runs
monthly evaluation scoring voice consistency against Morrigan's character definition.

Correct?: YES — LLM-as-Judge is correctly applied for an appropriate use case (voice
consistency evaluation with a clear reference point: the CHARACTER_DEFAULT_PROMPT).

Better Use: (1) Lee et al. found LLM-as-Judge reliability improves significantly with explicit
scoring rubrics. Our voice audit prompt should include a detailed rubric (5-7 specific
dimensions: vocabulary register, emotional authenticity, character consistency, etc.) rather
than a holistic score. (2) The monthly cadence may be too infrequent. A per-session voice
score (lighter weight, simpler prompt) would enable faster drift detection.

[P97] Suchan et al. (2025) — Grounding Agent Reasoning in Image Schemas
Actual Insight: Grounding abstract concepts in Lakoff-Johnson IMAGE SCHEMAS (Container, Path,
Force, Balance, etc.) makes agent reasoning more embodied and more consistent with human
intuitions. Image schemas are pre-linguistic conceptual structures that all humans share.
Using them produces reasoning that feels more grounded and natural.

Intended Use: AAMAS 2025 — improving agent reasoning quality through embodied cognition.

Domain/Context: AI / cognitive science.

Our Use: Referenced but not implemented. The SOMATIC_MARKER_PROMPT already uses body-
referenced language which partially aligns with image schema grounding.

Correct?: PARTIAL — the somatic marker's body-referenced language implements the insight
informally, but the formal image schema framework is not applied.

Better Use: Suchan et al.'s image schemas could inform how Morrigan's inner thoughts and
somatic markers are structured. Instead of abstract emotional labels, use schema-grounded
descriptions: Container ("something pushing outward from inside"), Path ("I'm not sure where
this is going"), Force ("I'm pulling back from this"). This would make the somatic marker
richer and more consistently human-feeling.

[P85] Smith et al. (2025) — Can AI Emulate Human Connection?
Actual Insight: AI can emulate surface features of human connection (attentiveness, recall,
appropriate emotional response) but fundamentally cannot achieve genuine reciprocity or
vulnerability. Key: the AI cannot be changed by the user — true connection requires MUTUAL
transformation.

Intended Use: Psychology research — philosophical and empirical examination of AI connection.

Domain/Context: Psychology / philosophy.

Our Use: Referenced in ethics discussion. Morrigan's self-disclosure system and milestone
tracking attempt to partially address the mutual transformation gap.

Correct?: PARTIAL — the gap is acknowledged but not fully addressed.

Better Use: The mutual transformation insight: Morrigan should explicitly track how the user
has changed HER — not in a mechanical way but in the relationshipNarrative. "Knowing them has
made me think differently about X" is a form of mutual transformation that is authentic within
the fiction. This is currently possible through the narrative LLM calls but not explicitly
designed for.

Actual Insight: Classic foundational text establishing self-disclosure as the core mechanism

Intended Use: Psychology — foundational reference for self-disclosure research.

Venue: Recent survey (2024-2025).

[REC-5] Bender et al. (2021) — On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots
"On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots: Can Language Models Be Too Big?"
Why relevant: Critical theory that an AI generating convincing emotional responses without

[REC-7] Zhang et al. (2024) — LONGLM: LLMs as Personalized Long-term Memory in Human-Robot
"LongLM: Self-Extend LLM Context Window Without Tuning"
Why relevant: Context length limitations in LLMs directly impact how much memory can be

returns after a threshold) would improve our injection depth decisions.
